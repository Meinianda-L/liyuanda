Balancing STEM and Arts

My experience as both a robotics competitor and a clarinet player - finding harmony between technology and music.

People often ask me how I balance my time between robotics competitions and music practice. To me, these two worlds aren't in conflict - they complement each other in ways I never expected.

The Structure of Music

Playing the clarinet has taught me discipline and precision. Every note must be hit perfectly, every rhythm must be exact. This attention to detail translates directly to my work in robotics, where a single miscalculation can mean the difference between victory and defeat.

The Creativity of Engineering

Conversely, robotics has taught me to think outside the box. When a mechanism isn't working, I need to find creative solutions. This creative problem-solving has made me a better musician, helping me interpret pieces with more nuance and expression.

"The best engineers are artists, and the best artists are engineers."

Time Management

Of course, balancing both requires careful time management. I typically spend my mornings on academics, afternoons on robotics, and evenings on music practice. Weekends are for competitions and performances.

The key is passion. When you love what you do, finding time becomes natural. Both robotics and music bring me joy, and I couldn't imagine giving up either.
